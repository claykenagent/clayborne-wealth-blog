# Cheapest Stock Screeners That Actually Work: Free vs Paid Tools (TradingView, Finviz, Thinkorswim Ranked)

You're staring at your brokerage account, ready to find your next trade. But first, you need to filter through thousands of stocks to find the few that actually match your strategy.

So you google "stock screener," and suddenly you're drowning in options. Some promise the moon for $500/month. Others are completely free but look like they were built in 2005. You're paralyzed, wondering: *Will I waste money on a tool I'll abandon in three months? Or will I cripple myself with a free screener that can't do what I need?*

Here's the truth most traders won't admit: **Most traders pick the wrong tool and blame their strategy.** They either overpay for enterprise-grade software they never use, or they cheap out on a free tool that's missing crucial filters—and then they wonder why their edge isn't working.

I've tested the major screeners so you don't have to. Let me show you exactly which one matches your trading style, your budget, and your actual needs.

## The Real Cost of Choosing Wrong

Before we rank the tools, let's talk about what happens when you pick poorly.

**Scenario 1: You go all-in on a $500/month premium screener.**
You commit. It's fancy. It has 47 filters you don't understand. You use 3 of them. After six months of paying $3,000 and still getting mediocre results, you realize the problem wasn't the tool—it was your lack of a real trading plan. Now you're out three grand and more discouraged than before.

**Scenario 2: You use a free screener to save money.**
It works fine for basic stuff. But when your strategy evolves and you need custom indicators, alerts, or integration with your broker, you hit a wall. You can't do what you want. So you waste time building workarounds, lose trades while you're tinkering, and eventually give up.

**The real cost isn't just money—it's time, opportunity, and confidence.**

If you're new to screening stocks, overpaying is a trap. If you're experienced, underpaying costs you in lost productivity. The goal is simple: **Find the cheapest tool that does exactly what you need, nothing more.**

## The Free Screeners That Actually Work

### **Finviz Free — Best for Beginners**

**Price:** Completely free (with ads)  
**Best for:** Swing traders, growth investors, fundamentals-focused traders

Finviz is the gold standard of free screeners. It's not fancy, but it does the job better than screeners that cost $50/month.

You get:
- 70+ stock filters (valuation, technical, financial health)
- Visual heat maps and correlation tools
- Pre-built scans for common strategies
- Clean, intuitive interface

**Why it works for you:** If you're starting out, Finviz teaches you exactly which filters matter. You won't waste money learning on expensive software. The catch? No alerts, no API for automation, and the free tier limits some advanced features. But honestly, if you can't profit with Finviz's free filters, a more expensive tool won't help.

**Free trial?** No trial needed—it's free forever. Upgrade to Finviz Elite ($40/month) only when you've outgrown the free version.

---

### **TradingView — Best Community Tool**

**Price:** Free with premium tiers ($15-60/month)  
**Best for:** Technical traders, chart lovers, strategy backesters

TradingView is where traders hang out. Yes, it's primarily charting software, but the screener is underrated.

You get (free):
- Basic stock screening by technical filters
- Pine Script for custom indicators (if you can code)
- 5,000+ community-built indicators
- Powerful charting tools
- Free alerts

**Why it works for you:** TradingView's real strength is combining screening with charting. You find a stock that matches your criteria, and boom—you're already looking at a professional chart. No switching between tools. Plus, the community aspect means you can steal strategies that other traders have already validated.

**Free trial?** Free forever, but some alerts and indicator limits apply. Upgrade to Premium ($15+/month) for unlimited alerts and indicators.

---

### **Yahoo Finance Stock Screener — Best Simplicity**

**Price:** Free  
**Best for:** Value investors, dividend hunters, buy-and-hold folks

Yahoo Finance's screener is ridiculously simple but remarkably useful for fundamental screening.

You get:
- Basic screening by market cap, P/E, dividend yield
- Pre-built screens for value stocks, dividend stocks, ETFs
- Data sourced directly from SEC filings
- One-click access to full company reports

**Why it works for you:** If you only need 5-10 filters and you care about fundamentals more than technicals, Yahoo is more than enough. It's fast, clean, and you won't be distracted by features you don't need.
