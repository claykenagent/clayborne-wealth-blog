## The Agitation: What Happens If You Wait?

Let's be honest about the opportunity cost of staying in cash.

**Scenario 1: You Do Nothing**
- You keep your $12 in a savings account earning 0.01% APY
- In 10 years, your $12 becomes $12.01
- Inflation means it's actually worth less

**Scenario 2: You Invest $12 into a Market-Tracking ETF (7% average annual return)**
- Your $12 becomes roughly $23.60 in 10 years
- You've nearly *doubled* your money by doing almost nothing

That's the difference between staying frozen and taking one small action.

But here's the deeper fear: *what if I'm making the wrong choice between ETFs and stocks?* What if you pick an ETF that underperforms? What if you pick a stock that collapses?

The truth? Both fears are valid—but **both are also overblown** when you're working with $12 and fractional shares.

## The Solution: Your Complete Comparison

### How Fractional Shares Changed Everything

Five years ago, you couldn't buy a stock worth $150 with just $12. Now, thanks to fractional share technology, you can own a piece of any stock on the market—regardless of its price.

**This changes everything.**

You're no longer limited to penny stocks or ultra-cheap companies. You can now build the exact portfolio you want, starting with $12.

### **Individual Dividend Stocks**

**What They Are:** You buy actual shares of real companies. You own a tiny piece of that business.

## The Honest Truth: Which One Wins?

**If you have $12 and you're a beginner:** Start with a micro-cap dividend ETF.

**Why?** Because at this capital level, you need the emotional guardrails that come with diversification. One bad stock pick doesn't tank your portfolio. You're learning how the market works without emotional devastation.

**If you have $50+ and you're willing to learn:** You can split your money—$25 into an ETF, $25 into a carefully researched individual stock.
