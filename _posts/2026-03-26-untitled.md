# The Best Weighted Blankets for Better Sleep: 2024 Buyer's Guide

If you've been struggling to fall asleep or stay asleep through the night, a weighted blanket might be the solution you've been searching for. These innovative bedding products have gained significant popularity over the past few years, and for good reason. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore everything you need to know about weighted blankets and help you find the perfect one for your needs.

## What Is a Weighted Blanket and How Does It Work?

A weighted blanket is a specially designed bed covering that contains materials (typically plastic pellets or glass beads) distributed throughout to add significant weight. Unlike regular blankets, weighted blankets typically range from 5 to 30 pounds, providing gentle pressure across your entire body.

The science behind weighted blankets is based on a concept called **deep pressure stimulation (DPS)**. This therapeutic technique is believed to activate the parasympathetic nervous system, which is responsible for relaxation and rest. When you use a weighted blanket, the pressure triggers the release of serotonin and melatonin—hormones that regulate mood and sleep—while reducing cortisol levels, the stress hormone.

Research suggests that weighted blankets can help with:
- Insomnia and sleep disorders
- Anxiety and stress
- ADHD symptoms
- Autism spectrum disorder
- Restless leg syndrome

## How to Choose the Right Weight

Selecting the correct weight for your weighted blanket is crucial for maximizing its benefits. The general rule of thumb is to choose a blanket that weighs approximately **10% of your body weight**, plus 1-2 pounds.

For example:
- If you weigh 150 pounds, a 15-17 pound blanket would be ideal
- If you weigh 200 pounds, aim for a 20-22 pound blanket
- If you weigh 100 pounds, a 10-12 pound blanket is appropriate

If you're purchasing a weighted blanket for a child, opt for the lighter end of the spectrum and ensure it's age-appropriate. Most experts recommend weighted blankets only for children over three years old.

## Key Features to Consider

### Material and Fabric Quality

The outer cover of your weighted blanket significantly impacts comfort and durability. Look for breathable, soft materials like cotton, bamboo, or weighted blanket-specific fabrics. These materials help regulate temperature and prevent overheating during the night.

### Fill Material

Weighted blankets use different fill materials, each with distinct advantages:

**Plastic pellets** are the most affordable option and provide even weight distribution. However, they can shift over time and may feel less luxurious.

**Glass beads** are premium, durable, and maintain their position better than pellets. They also tend to be quieter and provide smoother pressure distribution.

**Organic materials** like sand or natural fibers appeal to eco-conscious consumers but may be heavier for their weight.

### Temperature Regulation

If you live in a warm climate or tend to sleep hot, prioritize blankets with cooling features. Some premium weighted blankets include cooling gel layers or moisture-wicking fabrics that help dissipate heat.

### Washability

Check whether the cover is removable and machine-washable. This feature is essential for maintaining cleanliness and extending the blanket's lifespan.

## Weighted Blankets vs. Regular Blankets: Why Make the Switch?

Standard blankets simply provide warmth, while weighted blankets offer therapeutic benefits beyond temperature regulation. Users report falling asleep faster, experiencing deeper sleep, and waking up feeling more refreshed. The gentle pressure mimics the comfort of being held, which is particularly beneficial for people with anxiety or sensory processing issues.

## Tips for Using Your Weighted Blanket

**Start gradually**: If you're new to weighted blankets, begin by using it for short periods to acclimate your body.

**Position it correctly**: Keep the blanket evenly distributed across your body for maximum pressure stimulation.

**Choose the right bedding**: Use your weighted blanket with comfortable sheets and pillows to enhance overall sleep quality.

**Consider your sleeping position**: Weighted blankets work well for all sleeping positions, but side sleepers may find them particularly beneficial.

**Pair with sleep hygiene practices**: For best results, combine your weighted blanket with other healthy sleep habits like maintaining a consistent schedule and keeping your bedroom cool and dark.
