## The Hidden Fee Trap: Why Most Day Traders Lose Money Before They Even Start

Here's what happens when you trade micro-caps on the wrong broker:

**Commission fees** eat into your gains. A $100 profit gets cut to $75 after fees. That trade needed to be 33% more profitable just to break even on the transaction costs.

**Margin rates** compound the damage. Borrow $5,000 at 7.5% annual interest, and you're paying $312.50 a year—or $26 per month—just for the privilege of using the broker's money. On a micro-cap trade that only nets $40 profit, you're underwater before you finish your coffee.

**Account minimums** lock you out entirely. Some brokers require $25,000 minimum to day trade. If you have $15,000, you're excluded from the strategies that could actually grow your account.

**Hidden fees** disguise themselves as "regulatory fees," "exchange charges," or "data subscriptions." You don't see them clearly because they're buried in the fine print. But they're there, draining 2-4% of your trading capital annually.

The traders who get ahead aren't necessarily smarter—they're just aware. They've done the math. They know exactly how much they're paying, and they've chosen platforms that align with their budget and trading frequency.

If you haven't done this audit, you're essentially playing poker with money leaking out of your pocket. Every trade is starting from a deficit.
