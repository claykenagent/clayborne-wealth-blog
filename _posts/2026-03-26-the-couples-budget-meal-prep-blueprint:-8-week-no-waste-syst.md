## Part 1: The Container Selection Framework

This might seem boring, but it's the foundation. Wrong containers = waste. Right containers = consistency.

### The Three-Container System

Most couples fail because they try one type of container for everything. Instead, use three categories:

**1. Daily Containers (Glass, 32-40 oz)**
- Use for: Lunches, planned leftovers
- Why glass: Microwave-safe, dishwasher-safe, doesn't retain odors, lasts 5+ years
- Best options: [Pyrex Simply Store glass containers](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B01GZYJX2U?tag=thenestlovely-20) or [Rubbermaid Brilliance](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B00LEKB4NC?tag=thenestlovely-20)
- Cost per container: $3-5
- Buy: 8-10 containers (4-5 per person)

**2. Freezer Containers (Plastic, various sizes)**
- Use for: Batch-cooked grains, proteins, sauces
- Why plastic: Lightweight, stackable, freeze-friendly
- Best options: [Sistema To Go](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0BS4XWVVL?tag=thenestlovely-20) or [Glad Shallow Food Storage](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B08XBCF7VZ?tag=thenestlovely-20)
- Cost per container: $1-2
- Buy: 6-8 containers

**3. Portion Containers (Clear plastic compartment, 3-compartment)**
- Use for: Balanced plates ready to eat
- Why compartments: Natural portion control, prevents ingredients from mixing
- Best options: [Rubbermaid Take Alongs](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0B4K8HBXD?tag=thenestlovely-20) or [Bentgo Fresh](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B09KCPKPRT?tag=thenestlovely-20)
- Cost per container: $2-3
- Buy: 4-6 containers (use as needed)

**Total startup cost: $40-60 for a complete system**

### The Container Audit Checklist

Before you buy anything, audit what you have:
- [ ] Do you have broken/stained containers? Toss them.
- [ ] Do you have containers without matching lids? Donate or recycle.
- [ ] Do you have too many takeout containers? Keep 2-3 max, donate the rest.
- [ ] Do you have containers that don't fit in your freezer/fridge? Not usable.

**New containers only replace what was broken or missing.** This prevents the accumulation spiral.

## Part 2: The 8-Week Prep Template System

Don't overthink this. The pattern repeats every two weeks, giving you variety without complexity.

### Week 1 & 5: Protein + Grain Focus

**Sunday Prep (2 hours for 2 people)**

Batch cook:
- 3 lbs chicken breast (season with: salt, pepper, garlic powder)
- 4 cups brown rice
- 2 lbs ground turkey (for separate meals)
- 3 cups roasted vegetables (broccoli, carrots, sweet potato)

**Container assignments:**
- Daily containers: Chicken + rice + vegetables (2 portions/day × 5 days = 10 containers)
- Freezer containers: Ground turkey divided into 4 portions
- Compartment containers: Optional for grab-and-go

**Flexibility note:** One person might prefer the chicken as salad topping instead of with rice. Use freezer containers to store extra rice separately so they can customize.

### Week 2 & 6: Sheet Pan + Slow Cooker Week

**Wednesday Prep (90 minutes)**

- Sheet pan #1: Salmon + asparagus + lemon
- Sheet pan #2: Pork tenderloin + Brussels sprouts + olive oil
- Slow cooker: Beef chili (set it Sunday morning, freeze portions Wednesday)

**Why mid-week prep?** Fresh sheet pan meals for Wed-Fri keep enthusiasm high.

### Week 3 & 7: Breakfast + Snack Week

**Sunday Prep (60 minutes)**

- 12 egg muffins (freeze in compartment containers)
- 6 cups overnight oats (distributed into individual containers)
- 10 cups homemade granola
- Chopped vegetables for snacking

### Week 4 & 8: "Clean Out the Freezer" Week

**No formal prep.** Use what you've frozen. This prevents accumulation and forces eating what you've made.

## Part 4: The Storage Rotation Strategy

The #1 cause of food waste in couples: "I forgot what was in that container."

**Solve it with The FIFO System (First In, First Out):**

1. **Label everything with dates** (use a label maker or masking tape)
2. **Front-facing storage:** Oldest containers at eye level, newest in back
3. **Daily inventory (10 seconds):** Open fridge, see what expires first
4. **Freezer organization:** Group by protein type, use a simple inventory list on your fridge
