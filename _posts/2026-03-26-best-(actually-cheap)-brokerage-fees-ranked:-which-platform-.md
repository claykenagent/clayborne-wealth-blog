## The Problem: How Brokers Extract Money from Small Accounts

Let's be honest: traditional brokers don't want your $200 account.

Their business model was built on people with $100K portfolios paying $10 per trade. When that made sense, they charged accordingly. But in 2024, "commission-free trading" became table stakes.

So they got creative.

**Here's how they still get paid:**

- **Per-trade fees** on options, forex, or crypto (buried in the terms)
- **Minimum deposit requirements** that lock you out entirely
- **Inactivity fees** that penalize you for not trading every month
- **Bid-ask spreads** on forex (sometimes 1–3%)
- **Account closure fees** if you quit
- **Transfer-out fees** when you leave

For a $200 account, even a single $5 fee is 2.5% of your capital. Over a year, three trades × $5 = $15. That's 7.5% of your original investment *gone*. That's not trading—that's feeding the house.

## The Breakdown: 6 Brokers Ranked by Real-World Cost

### 🥇 Best Overall: Interactive Brokers

**Why it wins for micro-accounts:**
- Zero per-trade commissions on stocks and ETFs
- $0 minimum deposit (yes, really—you can open an account with nothing)
- No inactivity fees
- Lowest options costs (tier-based: $0.65 per contract with $0 base)
- Excellent forex spreads (0.2 pips on major pairs)

**The catch:** Interface is complex. Not beginner-friendly. Customer service can feel cold.

**Best for:** Serious traders who want maximum control and lowest fees. You'll spend 2 hours learning the platform—and save $100+ in the first year.

---

### 🥈 Best for Beginners: Webull

**Why it wins for new investors:**
- Zero commissions on stocks, ETFs, options
- No minimum deposit
- Clean, mobile-first interface
- Paper trading (practice risk-free)
- No inactivity fees
- Fractional shares starting at $1

**The catch:** Smaller company. Less established than Fidelity or E*TRADE. Some features are less polished.

**Best for:** First-time traders who want simplicity without paying for it. Want to buy fractional shares? Webull is your friend.

---

### 🥉 Best for Broad Access: Fidelity

**Why it wins:**
- Zero commissions across stocks, ETFs, options, futures
- $0 minimum deposit
- Research and educational tools are *excellent*
- No inactivity fees
- Excellent customer service (24/5 phone support)

**The catch:** Fractional shares cost $1 each (minor). Slightly higher bid-ask spreads than Interactive Brokers on forex.

**Best for:** People who want world-class support without sacrificing low fees. If you're nervous about starting, Fidelity's reputation is worth the peace of mind.

---

### 4️⃣ Best for Simplicity: Robinhood

**Why it works:**
- Zero commissions everywhere
- Fractional shares ($1 minimum)
- Incredibly intuitive mobile app
- $0 minimum deposit

**The catch:** Gamification can encourage overtrading. Options approval is quick (sometimes *too* quick). Higher spreads on forex and crypto.

**Best for:** People who want to make their first trade in 90 seconds. Social features work for some, distract others.

---

### 5️⃣ E*TRADE

**Solid middle ground:**
- Zero commissions on stocks/ETFs/options
- Options: $0.65 per contract
- $0 minimum deposit
- Good research tools
- Better educational content than Robinhood

**The catch:** Not as beginner-friendly as Webull. Spreads are wider than IB. Not much to differentiate it anymore.

**Best for:** People who've heard of E*TRADE and trust the brand. Perfectly fine—just not exceptional for small accounts.

---

### 6️⃣ TD Ameritrade

**Solid traditional choice:**
- Zero commissions (stocks/ETFs)
- Options: $0.65 per contract
- Powerful platform (thinkorswim)
- Excellent research

**The catch:** Owned by Charles Schwab now. Consolidation is ongoing. Spreads not as tight as Interactive Brokers. For most small traders, overkill.

**Best for:** People who want advanced charting and analysis tools.
