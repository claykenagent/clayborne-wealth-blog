# The Complete Trading Decision Journal: Stop Emotional Trading and Track What Actually Works

If you're like most traders, you've had this experience: You make a trade that feels right in the moment. Your conviction is high. Then the market moves against you, and suddenly you're questioning everything. You close the position at a loss, only to watch it reverse in your favor hours later.

**The worst part?** You can't remember *why* you entered that trade in the first place.

This is the trader's paradox. We make hundreds of micro-decisions—some brilliant, some disastrous—but we rarely document them. Without a record, we can't learn. Without learning, we repeat the same mistakes.

The solution isn't fancy software or a trading algorithm. It's a **Trading Decision Journal**—and I'm going to show you exactly how to build one that actually works.

---

## Why Traders Need a Decision Journal (Not Just a Trade Log)

There's a critical difference between a **trade log** and a **decision journal**.

A trade log records *what happened*: entry price, exit price, profit/loss.

A decision journal records *why it happened*: your thesis, your conviction level, the setup you saw, what surprised you, what you'd do differently.

Research by Nassim Taleb, Annie Duke (author of *Thinking in Bets*), and the entire field of behavioral finance confirms this: **traders who journal their decisions outperform those who don't.**

Why? Because journaling forces you to:

1. **Clarify your thesis before entering** – No more "it just feels right" trades
2. **Identify patterns in your losses** – Are you overtrading on low-conviction setups? Revenge trading after losses? Holding losers too long?
3. **Separate luck from skill** – Over time, you see which edge is actually yours
4. **Reduce emotional trading** – Writing forces deliberate thinking instead of reactive trading

## The Complete Trading Decision Journal Template

I've built three versions of this template below—one optimized for day traders, one for swing traders, and one for options traders. You can implement this in Google Sheets (free) or Excel, and use it as a PDF checklist before every trade.

### **SECTION 1: PRE-TRADE DECISION CHECKLIST**

Complete this *before* you enter the trade. This is your decision journal's foundation.

```
TRADE ENTRY CHECKLIST
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TRADE ID: [Unique identifier - e.g., "AAPL_021024_1"]
DATE: __________ TIME: __________ TICKER: __________

1. THESIS (Your conviction statement - 1-3 sentences)
   "I am entering this trade because..."
   ___________________________________________________________
   ___________________________________________________________

2. SETUP CLASSIFICATION (Check one)
   ☐ Support/Resistance Bounce
   ☐ Breakout (above resistance/below support)
   ☐ Trend Continuation
   ☐ Mean Reversion
   ☐ Technical Pattern (Specify: ___________________)
   ☐ Fundamental Catalyst (Specify: ___________________)
   ☐ Other: _________________

3. CONVICTION LEVEL (1-10, where 10 = "I'm certain")
   Conviction: _____/10

4. POSITION SIZING DECISION
   Account Size: $__________
   Risk Per Trade (%): _____% = $__________
   Position Size: __________ shares/contracts
   
   (Rule: Never risk more than 2% per trade. 1% is better.)

5. ENTRY POINT
   Entry Price: $__________
   Entry Method: ☐ Market Order ☐ Limit Order
   Why this price? _________________________________________

6. STOP LOSS POINT (Your max loss before exit)
   Stop Loss Price: $__________
   Dollar Risk: $__________
   
   Rule check: Is your stop loss LOGICAL (support level, 
   ATR-based, time-based) or ARBITRARY? 
   ☐ Logical ☐ Arbitrary (⚠️ Fix before trading)

7. PROFIT TARGET(s)
   Target 1: $__________ (Initial Target)
   Target 2: $__________ (If breakout occurs)
   Target 3: $__________ (Max profit scenario)

8. EMOTIONAL STATE CHECK (Mandatory)
   ☐ Calm and analytical
   ☐ Slightly excited
   ☐ Aggressive/Overconfident
   ☐ Desperate (chasing after a loss)
   ☐ Distracted/Rushed
   
   ⚠️ If you checked anything other than "calm," WAIT 5 minutes
      or SKIP this trade.

9. RISK/REWARD RATIO
   Potential Reward: $__________
   Maximum Risk: $__________
   R:R Ratio: 1:____ (Minimum target: 1:2)

10. EDGE STATEMENT (Why do YOU have an advantage?)
    "My edge in this trade is..."
    ___________________________________________________________
    ___________________________________________________________
```

### **SECTION 2: DURING-TRADE MONITORING LOG**

Use this if you're actively holding and need to track your mindset:

```
DURING TRADE - REAL-TIME LOG
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TIME: _______ PRICE: _______ PROFIT/LOSS: $_______

Current Emotion:
☐ Confident (Trade is working as planned)
☐ Uncomfortable (Price moved against me, testing conviction)
☐ Panicked (Want to exit immediately)
☐ Tempted (Tempted to move stops lower or ignore risk)

Price Action Update:
Is the trade performing as I expected? 
☐ Yes, thesis intact
☐ Partially (some targets hit, but not all)
☐ No, thesis broken (CONSIDER EXIT)

Action Taken This Check-In:
☐ Holding
☐ Moved stop to breakeven
☐ Took partial profits
☐ Exited position
☐ Added to position
☐ Adjusted target

Notes: _______________________________________________________
```

### **SECTION 3: POST-TRADE DEBRIEF (MOST IMPORTANT)**

Complete this *within 24 hours* of closing the trade. This is where the real learning happens.

```
POST-TRADE DEBRIEF
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TRADE ID: _________________ OUTCOME: Win / Loss / Breakeven

1. RESULTS
   Entry Price: $_________
   Exit Price: $_________
   Profit/Loss: $_________ (____% return)
   Reason for Exit: ____________________________________________

2. THESIS ACCURACY
   Did the trade move in the direction I predicted?
   ☐ Yes, exactly as expected
   ☐ Partially (right direction, wrong timing)
   ☐ No, thesis was completely wrong

   If no, what surprised you?
   ___________________________________________________________

3. EXECUTION QUALITY (Rate 1-10)
   ☐ Perfect execution (followed plan exactly)
   ☐ Good execution (minor deviations)
   ☐ Poor execution (deviated significantly)
   ☐ Terrible execution (ignored plan entirely)

   What did you do differently than planned?
   ___________________________________________________________

4. EMOTIONAL MANAGEMENT (Rate 1-10)
   How well did you manage emotions during this trade?
   
   Rating: _____/10
   
   ☐ Stayed disciplined
   ☐ Felt tempted to break rules but resisted
   ☐ Broke rules due to emotion (Specify: ___________________)

5. THE CRITICAL QUESTION: Did you win/lose because of SKILL or LUCK?
   
   ☐ SKILL (Your setup, thesis, and execution were sound)
   ☐ LUCK (You got lucky or unlucky; thesis was questionable)
   ☐ MIXED (Some skill, some luck involved)

   Evidence for your answer:
   ___________________________________________________________

6. KEY LEARNING
   "The most important thing I learned from this trade is..."
   ___________________________________________________________
   ___________________________________________________________

7. WHAT I'LL DO DIFFERENTLY NEXT TIME
   ___________________________________________________________
   ___________________________________________________________

8. PATTERN DETECTION
   Have I made similar trades before? How did those turn out?
   ___________________________________________________________
```
