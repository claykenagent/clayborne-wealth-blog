# Micro-Cap Momentum Trade Setup Checklist: The Complete Entry Validation System

Trading micro-cap stocks is thrilling—but it's also where most retail traders lose money. The difference between consistent winners and blown accounts isn't luck. It's **systematic entry validation**.

I've spent two years testing hundreds of micro-cap setups, tracking what works and what doesn't. This is the exact pre-trade checklist I use before entering *every single position*. No exceptions.

This isn't theory. It's the mechanical system that helps separate high-probability setups from noise.

## Why Most Micro-Cap Traders Fail at Entry

Here's the honest truth: 70% of traders I've worked with skip validation steps. They see a chart pattern, volume spike, or news catalyst and hit buy immediately. Then they watch their position bleed.

## The Complete Micro-Cap Momentum Trade Setup Checklist

Print this. Use it before *every* entry. Yes, every single one.

### **PHASE 1: SCANNER & WATCHLIST VALIDATION** ✓

Before you even look at a chart, does the stock meet basic criteria?

- [ ] **Market Cap Check**: $300M–$2B range (true micro-caps, below institutional heavy ownership)
- [ ] **Price Range**: $2–$15 per share (optimal for momentum, liquidity sweet spot)
- [ ] **Average Daily Volume (ADV)**: Minimum 500K shares
  - *Why?* Ensures you can exit position without slippage
- [ ] **Relative Volume (RV)**: Today's volume ≥ 1.5x the 20-day average
  - *What this means*: Abnormal activity = potential momentum catalyst
- [ ] **Float**: Under 50M shares (easier to move on volume)
- [ ] **Sector Check**: Not a biotech (too binary), not a penny stock penny-pincher (too risky)
- [ ] **No Major Dilution News**: Check last 10 filings for authorized share increases

**Real Example (Anonymized):**
- Stock XYZ: $8.50, 750K shares average daily volume, 3.2M float
- Today: 2.1M volume traded (2.8x RV)
- **Result**: Passes Phase 1 ✓

### **PHASE 2: TECHNICAL CHART PATTERN VALIDATION** ✓

Volume is on. Now confirm the chart pattern.

**Valid Momentum Entry Patterns** (pick ONE—don't mix):

#### **Pattern A: Breakout Above Resistance**
- [ ] Price breaks above clearly defined resistance level
- [ ] Resistance tested at least 2x in past 5 days
- [ ] Volume on breakout candle > 2x ADV (confirmation)
- [ ] Breakout occurs on daily or 4-hour timeframe (not 5-min noise)
- [ ] Prior pullback to resistance showed support (didn't crack below)

#### **Pattern B: Inverse Head & Shoulders**
- [ ] Left shoulder formed (clear low), right shoulder formed
- [ ] Head (middle valley) lower than both shoulders
- [ ] Neckline resistance identified with historical touches
- [ ] Breakout above neckline on volume
- [ ] Pattern took at least 10 days to form (rules out noise)

#### **Pattern C: VWAP Bounce on Daily**
- [ ] Price pulled back to Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP)
- [ ] Strong support at VWAP (bounced off it 1-2 times)
- [ ] Current candle: bullish reversal (green close, higher low than previous)
- [ ] Volume on reversal candle > ADV
- [ ] Resistance identified above (not buying into resistance)

#### **Pattern D: Consolidation Breakout**
- [ ] Stock traded sideways for 8-15 days (narrow range)
- [ ] Top of range clearly defined, bottom clearly defined
- [ ] Volume dried up during consolidation (compressed, waiting)
- [ ] Breakout candle: volume spike > 2x ADV, close above range
- [ ] Open interest or option flow confirms directional bias (optional confirmation)

**Real Example (Anonymized):**
- Stock ABC: Traded $5.80–$6.10 for 12 days
- Day 13: Opens at $6.00, closes $6.35 on 3.1M volume (vs. 900K ADV)
- **Result**: Valid consolidation breakout pattern ✓

### **PHASE 3: RISK/REWARD & POSITION SIZING VALIDATION** ✓

This is where most traders fail. They enter without knowing where they're exiting.

- [ ] **Stop-Loss Level Identified**: Clear technical level (beneath support, below low of pattern)
  - Typical stop placement: 2–4% below entry
- [ ] **Risk Per Trade**: Calculate = (Entry price − Stop price) × Position size
  - Cap this at 1% of total account (e.g., $500 risk on $50K account)
- [ ] **Profit Target Identified**: Use nearest resistance above (don't guess)
  - *How to find it*: Look at prior swing highs, volume nodes, technical levels
- [ ] **Risk/Reward Ratio ≥ 1:2**: Profit target distance ≥ 2x your stop loss distance
  - *Example*: Stop is $0.30 away, target is $0.60+ away
  - *Never* enter a trade where reward < 2x risk
- [ ] **Position Size Calculation**: Risk ÷ (Entry − Stop) = # of shares
  - *Example*: Want to risk $200, entry $10, stop $9.80 → buy ~667 shares (200 ÷ 0.20)
- [ ] **Account % Check**: Position size shouldn't exceed 3–5% of total account value
- [ ] **Exit Plan Written Down**: Know your profit target AND your stop BEFORE buying

**Real Example (Anonymized):**
- Stock DEF: Entry $7.00, Stop $6.70 (−$0.30 = 3% risk)
- Nearest resistance: $7.60 (profit target)
- Target distance: +$0.60 (risk/reward = 1:2) ✓
- Account size: $40K → Risk 1% = $400 max risk
- Position size: $400 ÷ $0.30 = 1,333 shares
- Dollar position: 1,333 × $7 = $9,331 (23% of account—actually high, scale to 5%)
- **Adjusted position: 267 shares ($1,869) = 1% account risk** ✓

### **PHASE 4: CATALYST & MOMENTUM CONFIRMATION** ✓

Why is this stock moving *today*? Don't ignore it.

- [ ] **Catalyst Identified** (check one):
  - [ ] Earnings report / earnings date announced
  - [ ] FDA approval / clinical trial data
  - [ ] Product launch / major partnership news
  - [ ] Insider buying (check Form 4 filings)
  - [ ] Short squeeze (high short float % + low float)
  - [ ] Sector rotation (entire sector moving up)
  - [ ] Unusual options activity (puts rising, calls increasing)
  - [ ] Technical breakout alone (no catalyst needed if setup is clean)
- [ ] **Catalyst Timing**: If scheduled event, confirm date. Don't enter day-before if stock likely to gap.
- [ ] **Sentiment Check**: 
  - StockTwits trending? (Not required, but informational)
  - Major financial news outlet covering it? (Legitimacy check)
  - Avoid pump-and-dump patterns (sudden 300% moves on no news)
- [ ] **IV Rank / Implied Volatility**: Check if abnormally high
  - High IV = larger moves expected (good for momentum)
  - Extreme IV (>90%) = possible reversal risk after move
