# The $5/Day Meal Prep Blueprint: Complete Guide to Budget Grocery Shopping & Portion Planning

Meal prep doesn't have to be complicated or expensive. In fact, with the right system, you can eat healthy, prepared meals for just $5 per day—or about $150/month for three meals. This blueprint breaks down exactly how to shop smarter, portion correctly, and track every penny.

## Why This System Works

Most people fail at budget meal prep because they:
- Buy ingredients without a plan
- Waste food through poor portioning
- Lose track of actual costs
- Get overwhelmed by complex recipes

This blueprint eliminates all three problems using a simple three-step system: **Plan → Calculate → Track**.

## Part 1: The Smart Shopping List Template

The foundation of $5/day eating is strategic shopping. You need a system that prevents impulse buys while ensuring nutritional balance.

### Core Budget Categories (By Cost-Per-Serving)

**Proteins ($0.50–$1.50/serving)**
- Eggs: $0.12–$0.18 per egg
- Canned tuna/chicken: $0.40–$0.60 per can
- Ground turkey: $2–$3/lb (makes 4–6 servings)
- Greek yogurt: $0.30–$0.50 per serving
- Dried beans/lentils: $0.08–$0.15 per serving (cooked)
- Rotisserie chicken: $6–$8 each (8–10 servings)

**Vegetables ($0.25–$0.75/serving)**
- Frozen vegetables (broccoli, carrots, mixed): $0.15–$0.35 per serving
- Seasonal fresh: bell peppers, zucchini, cabbage, spinach
- Canned tomatoes: $0.20–$0.35 per can
- Potatoes/sweet potatoes: $0.10–$0.25 per serving

**Grains & Carbs ($0.15–$0.40/serving)**
- Rice (bulk): $0.08–$0.12 per serving
- Oats (bulk): $0.10–$0.15 per serving
- Pasta: $0.15–$0.25 per serving
- Beans (dried, bulk): $0.08–$0.12 per serving
- Sweet potatoes: $0.15–$0.25 per serving

**Fats & Flavor ($0.10–$0.30/serving)**
- Olive oil: $0.05–$0.10 per serving
- Butter: $0.08–$0.12 per serving
- Soy sauce, vinegar, spices: $0.02–$0.05 per serving

## Part 2: The Portion Calculator Template

Overeating or under-portioning kills budget meals. This template takes the guesswork out.

### Standard Macro Portions (Per Meal)

- **Protein:** 4–6 oz (roughly your palm size)
- **Carbs:** 1/2–3/4 cup cooked (roughly your fist size)
- **Vegetables:** 1.5–2 cups (roughly two handfuls)
- **Fat:** 1–2 teaspoons cooking oil + natural fats from protein

### Sample Meal Plans (Hitting the $5/Day Target)

**Meal #1: Turkey & Rice Bowl**
- 5 oz ground turkey: $1.25
- 3/4 cup cooked rice: $0.18
- 1.5 cups broccoli: $0.30
- 1 tsp olive oil: $0.05
- **Total: $1.78/serving**

**Meal #2: Egg & Vegetable Scramble**
- 3 eggs: $0.45
- 1 cup spinach + peppers: $0.40
- 1/2 slice toast: $0.15
- 1 tsp butter: $0.08
- **Total: $1.08/serving**

**Meal #3: Tuna Pasta Salad**
- 1 can tuna (5 oz): $0.60
- 1 cup cooked pasta: $0.25
- 2 cups mixed vegetables: $0.50
- 2 tsp olive oil + vinegar: $0.10
- **Total: $1.45/serving**

**Daily Average: $4.31/day** (leaves room for snacks or higher-cost proteins)

## 5 Money-Saving Hacks

1. **Buy proteins on sale & freeze** — Check weekly ads before shopping
2. **Buy bulk grains/beans** — Cost drops 60% vs. packaged
3. **Go frozen for vegetables** — Just as nutritious, never spoils, cheaper
4. **Use the "root vegetable trick"** — Potatoes, carrots, onions last 3–4 weeks
5. **Prep in batches** — Cook 3 lbs ground
