## The Problem: Why Small Investors Get Squeezed

Let me paint a realistic scenario. Sarah has $1,000 to invest. She opens an account at a broker that charges $5 per trade. She buys 5 stocks over the first month to diversify—that's $25 in fees right there. Her $1,000 account just took a 2.5% hit before the market even moved.

Now, if those stocks grow at a modest 10% annually, she needs them to hit 10% *plus* overcome the fee drag. Over time, those small fees compound against you, not for you.

The real danger? Many brokers targeting beginners don't advertise their full fee structure upfront. You might discover months in that your "free" account has account maintenance fees, or that micro-cap stocks cost extra to trade.
