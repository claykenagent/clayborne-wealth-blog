# Budget Investing Apps Under $100 Starting Balance: Fidelity vs M1 vs Webull (2024)

You've got $12.29 in your checking account after groceries and gas. It's not much, but it's something. And you've been thinking: *What if I could make that money work for me instead of just sitting there?*

The frustrating truth? Traditional brokerages won't even talk to you with that balance. They want $500. $1,000. Sometimes $25,000 just to open a brokerage account. The system was literally built to keep people like you out.

But here's what's changed: fractional shares and commission-free trading have democratized investing. You don't need a trust fund anymore. You just need the right app.

In this guide, I'm comparing three platforms that actually welcome micro-investors—and I'll show you exactly why each one matters for your situation.

## The Real Problem: You're Being Locked Out of Wealth-Building

Let me be direct: not starting to invest because you don't have "enough" money is one of the most expensive decisions you can make.

Think about it this way. If you invested that $12.29 today and never added another penny, compounding would turn it into roughly $55 in 30 years (at 6% annual returns). Not life-changing, sure. But add $50 a month? That becomes $28,000+.

The problem isn't the amount. It's that most platforms were designed in an era when you needed a stockbroker and a suit to participate. Those gatekeepers are gone now, but the *feeling* that "you're too small to matter" remains.

This isn't just about money. It's about believing you deserve to participate in building wealth. Because you do.

**The stakes?** Every month you wait is a month of compound growth you'll never get back. You can't rewind time, but you can start today.

## The Solution: Three Apps That Actually Want Your Money (Even If It's $12)

### Fidelity: The Fortress of Beginner Investing

**Best for:** Newbies who want hand-holding and education alongside their investments.

Fidelity is the grandparent of retail investing. They've been around since 1946, and they treat small accounts with the same respect as large ones—no minimum deposit, no monthly fees, nothing to punish you for being small.

**Why it works for under $100:**
- Zero account minimum (literally open with $1)
- Commission-free stock and ETF trades
- Fractional shares starting at $1
- Exceptional educational resources (research tools, webinars, articles written for actual humans)
- Fidelity Go (their robo-advisor) available with no minimum

**The real win?** Fidelity's customer service is famously good. If you get confused, you can actually talk to a human who isn't trying to upsell you. That matters when you're learning.

**The friction:** Their app feels a bit corporate and dense compared to flashier competitors. It's not Instagram-pretty, but it works reliably.

**👉 [Open a Fidelity account here](https://www.fidelity.com)**

---

### M1 Finance: The Hands-Off Wealth Builder

**Best for:** People who want to set it and forget it, without paying for a financial advisor.

M1 Finance is built on a philosophy: automate your investing so you don't have to think about it (or panic during crashes).

**Why it works for under $100:**
- Zero minimum to start
- Pie portfolios (their version of pre-built investing plans) handle allocation for you
- Automatic rebalancing so you're not constantly tinkering
- Fractional shares built in
- No trading commissions, no advisory fees

**The real win?** You could literally deposit $20, pick an age-based portfolio pie (like "Age 25"), and forget about it for five years. The app does the heavy lifting.

**The friction:** Less beginner education than Fidelity. M1 assumes you either know what you're doing or want to be hands-off. Not ideal if you want to learn stock-picking.

**👉 [Start with M1 Finance here](https://www.m1finance.com)**

---

### Webull: The Trader's Playground

**Best for:** People who want to feel like they're actively managing their portfolio (even if they're starting small).

Webull appeals to people who like charts, stock data, and the feeling of control. It's a U.S. brokerage (founded in 2017) that's rapidly grown by offering tools usually reserved for advanced traders—but at beginner prices.

**Why it works for under $100:**
- Zero account minimum
- Commission-free trading on stocks, ETFs, and options
- Real-time market data and advanced charting (normally costs money)
- Fractional shares as low as $1
- Paper trading (practice with fake money first)

**The real win?** You get professional-grade research and charting tools without a $100/month subscription. Perfect if you want to learn by doing.

**The friction:** More bells and whistles can overwhelm true beginners. And Webull's customer support, while improving, isn't as legendary as Fidelity's.

**👉 [Sign up for Webull here](https://www.webull.com)**

## Level Up Your Investing Knowledge

Starting small doesn't mean thinking small. While you're building your first portfolio, invest in your financial education. These resources have helped thousands of beginners move from "investing is for rich people" to "I'm actually building wealth."

**Recommended Reading:**

1. **[The Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0060555661?tag=thenestlovely-20)** — The bible of value investing. Dense but life-changing. Start here if you want to understand *why* you're investing, not just *how*.

2. **[A Random Walk Down Wall Street by Burton Malkiel](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0393352242?tag=thenestlovely-20)** — Makes the case for passive, long-term investing. Perfect counter-argument to day-trading fantasies.

3. **[Thinking, Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0374275637?tag=thenestlovely-20)** — Not strictly about investing, but it explains *why* your brain sabotages your money decisions. Game-changer.

4. **[The Bogleheads' Guide to Investing by Taylor Larson et al.](https://www.amazon.com/dp/0470067365?tag=thenestlovely-20)** — Practical, beginner-friendly, and doesn't try to make you a day trader. Highly recommended.

5. **[Physical Investment Ledger & Portfolio Tracker](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0BZ5RQ1V5?tag=thenestlovely-20)** — Some people find tracking investments by hand (even alongside apps) makes them more intentional. Worth considering
