## Part 1: The Psychology of Investing (Why Most People Never Start)

Before we talk money, we need to talk *mind*.

**The biggest barrier to investing isn't lack of capital—it's lack of clarity.** Most beginners feel paralyzed by information overload. Should I buy individual stocks? ETFs? Crypto? Real estate? The overwhelm wins, and they don't invest at all.

Here's what separates successful investors from the broke:

**Principle 1: Start Before You're Ready**

There's no "perfect time" to invest. Market timing is a myth. Someone who invested $1K on January 1st, 2008 (the worst possible timing in 20 years) would still be up 600%+ by 2024. The year you start matters less than the year you start mattering.

**Principle 2: Simplicity Beats Complexity**

Warren Buffett tells most investors to buy index funds. Not because it's exciting—because it works. Your job isn't to beat the market. Your job is to capture market returns with minimal fees and minimal effort.

**Principle 3: The Compound Effect is Real**

$1K invested at 8% annual returns becomes:
- $2,160 in 10 years
- $4,660 in 20 years  
- $10,060 in 30 years

You didn't add another dollar. Compound interest did the heavy lifting. This is why time in market beats timing the market.

Here's your template. Copy it:

├── Stock Index Funds: 80% ($800)
│   ├── US Total Market ETF (VTI or FSKAX): 60% ($600)

│   └── Total Bond Market ETF (BND or FXNAX): 20% ($200)

**Why This Allocation?**

- **VTI or FSKAX ($600):** Gives you ownership in ~3,500 US companies. Your core holding.
- **VXUS or FTIHX ($200):** International diversification. The global economy isn't just America.
- **BND or FXNAX ($200):** Bonds reduce volatility. When stocks fall 20%, bonds usually stay flat or rise.

**The 80/20 Split:** Aggressive enough to generate real returns, conservative enough to sleep at night.

## Part 5: Tracking and Rebalancing (Boring But Important)

Once you invest, you might feel tempted to check your portfolio daily. Don't.

**The Right Frequency:**
- Check quarterly (every 3 months) ✅
- Check daily (recipe for panic selling) ❌

**Rebalancing Rule:**
Once per year (or if any holding drifts 5%+ from target), rebalance back to your original allocation.

Example: If stocks soar and your allocation becomes 85% stocks / 15% bonds, sell some stocks and buy bonds to return to 80/20.

**Where to Track:** Fidelity's dashboard does this automatically. No spreadsheets needed.

## Part 6: The Five Mistakes You'll Avoid (Because I'm Telling You)

**Mistake #1: Panic Selling During Downturns**
Markets drop 10-20% regularly. This is normal. You only lose money if you sell. Don't sell.

**Mistake #2: Trying to Time the Market**
"I'll invest when interest rates drop." No. You won't. Invest now.

**Mistake #3: Chasing Performance**
You see crypto up 500% and feel FOMO. Index funds returned "only" 10%. The index fund investor sleeps. You don't. Which is better?

**Mistake #4: Paying High Fees**
Your entire allocation should cost <0.20% per year in fees. That's $2 on every $1,000. Higher than that? You're getting scammed.

**Mistake #5: Not Increasing Your Contributions**
$1K is great. But $1K + $100/month for 20 years becomes $28K invested, worth $95K+. The monthly habit matters more than the initial lump sum.
